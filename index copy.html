Heart Postheses

Developing a novel polymer aortic valve prosthesis

Heart valve diseases are a common and rising health concern, affecting one in eight people in the UK. Treatment often necessitates prosthetic interventions, which impose a substantial financial burden on the national health service, and have wide ranging implications on patients' lives.
Existing prosthetics face two key challenges: Prosthetics based on biological materials, such as pig or cow tissue, have good biocompatibility but often lack the required durability to last for the entirety of a patient's life. Mechanical prosthetics on the other hand, which are often carbon or titanium based, last for life but have biocompatibility issues. To mitigate these, patients must take daily blood thinning medication to prevent blood clots, posing further health risks.
Recent advancements in (co)polymer materials have shown that such materials can exhibit both excellent biocompatibility and durability, possibly placing within reach the development of a prosthetic, which solves the biocompatibility-durability trade off.

Knee Stabilization Surgery

Finite element study to deduce optimal operative procedure which minimises fracture risk during knee surgery

This exciting biomechanical project formed my Engineering Master Thesis at the University of Cambridge, through which I won the Sir George Nelson Prize in Applied Mechanics (top mark in Mechanical Engineering).
Arthritis of the knee affects one in five adults aged over 45 in England, and is the primary reason for knee replacement surgeries. Tibial osteotomy surgery is an alternative, longer-lasting treatment which preserves the knee joints, rather than replacing them. However, this surgery involves the risk of bone fracture, and current surgical procedures may not be as effective as they could be to prevent fracture.
I conducted a finite element study and experiments with a self-assembled rig to determine how variation in intra-operative surgical procedures affect the likelihood and severity of bone fracture
