Knee Stabilization Surgery

Finite element study to deduce optimal operative procedure which minimises fracture risk during knee surgery

2021 - 2022 Jane Doe

This exciting biomechanical project formed my Engineering Master Thesis at the University of Cambridge, through which I won the Sir George Nelson Prize in Applied Mechanics (top mark in Mechanical Engineering).

Arthritis of the knee affects one in five adults aged over 45 in England, and is the primary reason for knee replacement surgeries. Tibial osteotomy surgery is an alternative, longer-lasting treatment which preserves the knee joints, rather than replacing them. However, this surgery involves the risk of bone fracture, and current surgical procedures may not be as effective as they could be to prevent fracture.
I conducted a finite element study and experiments with a self-assembled rig to determine how variation in intra-operative surgical procedures affect the likelihood and severity of bone fracture
